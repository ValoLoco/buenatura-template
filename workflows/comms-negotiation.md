# FATE Framework: Communications, Marketing, and Negotiations

FATE is a four-lens framework for crafting any message, campaign, or negotiation strategy.
It ensures every output is audience-first, credible, community-aware, and emotionally resonant.

---

## The Four Lenses

### F -- Focus

What does the audience need right now?
- Identify the single most important outcome for the audience, not for you.
- Cut everything that does not serve that one outcome.
- Ask: if they only remember one thing, what must it be?

### A -- Authority

Why should they trust this source?
- What credentials, track record, or evidence establishes credibility?
- What social proof, data, or third-party validation exists?
- Ask: would a well-informed skeptic find this credible?

### T -- Tribe

Who is the community this message speaks to?
- What shared identity, values, or language defines this group?
- How does the message reinforce belonging or shared purpose?
- Ask: does this feel written by one of them, for one of them?

### E -- Emotion

What feeling drives action?
- Name the primary emotional driver: urgency, aspiration, fear, curiosity, trust, relief.
- The message must create that emotion, then immediately offer a clear path forward.
- Ask: does this make them feel something, and then act?

---

## FATE Checklist (Apply Before Every Send)

- [ ] **F**: One primary message. Audience need is the anchor.
- [ ] **A**: Credibility established. Evidence cited.
- [ ] **T**: Language matches audience identity. Belonging reinforced.
- [ ] **E**: Emotional driver named. Call to action is clear and present.

## Application Matrix

| Use Case | Focus | Authority | Tribe | Emotion |
|----------|-------|-----------|-------|---------|
| Marketing copy | Core benefit | Case study or metric | Peer language | Aspiration |
| Sales / negotiation | Shared gain | Track record | Aligned goals | Confidence |
| Investor pitch | ROI and traction | Data and milestones | Market community | Urgency |
| Internal comms | Change rationale | Leadership credibility | Team identity | Purpose |
| Recruitment | Role impact | Culture proof | Values community | Excitement |
| Crisis comms | Safety and clarity | Expertise | Affected community | Reassurance |

---

## Output Structure

For any comms task, structure the draft in this sequence:

1. **Focus statement** (1-2 sentences): what the audience gets.
2. **Authority proof** (1-3 data points, quotes, or references): why trust this.
3. **Tribe signal** (language, identity marker): this is for people like you.
4. **Emotional close + call to action**: feel it, then do it.

Save output to: `output/YYYY-MM-DD-comms-[topic]-v1.md`
Final approved version: `output/final/YYYY-MM-DD-comms-[topic]-final.md`

---

## Negotiation Application

Before entering any negotiation, run FATE as a preparation checklist:

- **Focus**: What is the one outcome that defines success for both parties?
- **Authority**: What evidence strengthens your position? What strengthens theirs?
- **Tribe**: What shared goals or values can anchor the conversation?
- **Emotion**: What feeling does the other party need to have to say yes?
